5. Project Ideas

Overview

My project, a Virtual Reality (VR) application system, aims to give its users the ability to customise and redesign their immediate their surroundings without the physical consequences of doing so. By avoiding the hassle of moving furniture then judge, they would be able to plan beforehand, making the whole process more efficient. I plan to open the product to users with both business and home-oriented intentions in mind, providing them the necessary tools to satisfy each their renovating needs. However, I must be wary of any drawbacks and unforeseen consequences that the system could bear and ensure the testing is rigorous and the feedback is recognised.

Motivation

According to one study done, 40% of participating Australians admitted to feeling " guilty, depressed, or anxious" about their house's clutter (Organized Interiors, 2019). Clearly, a significant number of people feel alienated from their own homes -- a sentiment no one should have to hold. In fact, I would include myself in that grouping, personally knowing how unruly it can be just to live with my own habits and shortcomings. This is why I'd like to offer these people an alternative -- an enticing opportunity to clean their house by planning how to do it beforehand. Research has shown that people found that "Disorganization (not lack of space) causes 80% of household clutter" (Organized Interiors, 2019); therefore, as they too have identified the root of their issues, I believe my VR product will be a relevant and effective solution to help their process.

Description

The fundamental aim of the product is to assist users in building their ideal room in the most time-efficient way possible. The actual VR experience will involve users putting on the VR set and either signing up or logging into their pre-existing account (through their VR controllers. Having successfully verified their credentials, the user will be presented with the 'main menu', where they will have the options of either proceeding to the main experience, accessing the settings or simply exiting the program. If users decide to access the main program, they will "appear" in a blank, white room. In here, they will be asked to replicate a room of their choosing by taking photos of it as a 360 degree panorama, either through the VR set's built in camera or through their phone's to be uploaded and accessed in their account's cloud storage. Either option will be just as effortless as the other.
With the data of the selected room, our system's artificial intelligence (AI) will analyse the images and reconstruct the room from it. If any images are misplaced, users will have the opportunity to move and change them until they choose otherwise.
Once users are satisfied with the room, the AI will re-examine the room, this time to distinguish the room's walls from any objects separate from it. It will then render the space into 3D to be manipulated by the user from their seats or moving around the relegated space. From here, they will be able to plan out how they want to fix their rooms with access to other 3D models of furniture from our databases. This will be organised and supplied by partnering with furniture companies such as IKEA, hopefully bolstering our database to be diverse enough.
Satisfied with their new choices, users can save their edited rooms to their account's cloud storage where they can access it later or delete it.

To ensure that this mission statement is met, engaging with the VR goggles should made accessible in several ways for users. Different models should be manufactured to target the specific needs of each group. For example, we could have separate models developed for consumer and business specific use. Consumer-specific models could be cheaper with the screen being replaced with a slot for users to insert their phones in, negating the issue of constantly jumping between one's phone the VR's screens when using the program. However, business models would be expected to be installed with the screens to avoid miscommunication between different operating systems and for purpose of keeping everyone on track.

Notably, the VR goggles must meet certain security necessities. As our program will store data entailing our consumers' homes and its rooms, we must guarantee that this data is properly stored against Distributed Denial of Service attacks (DDOS) or forms of spyware sneaking into the system. This could be completed through firewalls; logical securities such as passwords, restrictions, and audit trails; as well as physical securities like fingerprint scanners, video surveillance and barred windows.

Tools and Technologies

The product will bear almost all of the conventional aspects of VR goggles: a display screen, the housing case, the goggles, the head-strap, and the goggles or lenses. I intend for these aspects to be customisable to an extent, capable of addressing the user's needs when required. For example, the goggles' strap must be adjustable to accommodate for differing head sizes and overall comfortability, the display screen should be capable of changing brightness depending on the user's request, and the goggles must come equipped with cushions for long hours of usage.
Software could range from the employment of a simple yet functional operating system for the program and electronic display. A network connection will also be needed to connect the user's VR device and phone to the cloud storage to access their data via login.

Skills Required

Consumers will be expected to be familiar with using a VR device; though, in the situation in which they aren't, a step-by-step help manual is provided in the package. Youtube tutorials will also be made available to consumers. Software such as the aforementioned ones will need to be planned, coded, and tested for feedback prior to any consumer involvement. A team will have to be set up to work on said code as well as test groups to give relevant criticisms for the program's designs and functionality. Applicants for the development team will undergo a rigorous interviewing process to ensure they have the skills necessary to realising the project. Additionally, we will need to outsource our production of the actual VR goggles to another business, preferably one which will offer the lowest price with the best results in durability and longevity.

Outcome

To conclude, I would consider our company's key performance indicators when noting our success. A significantly low number of customer feedback would be an important one to indicate our overall customer's satisfaction with the product. Maintaining a large portion of the market share in our industry would also reflect our dominance over our competitors as consumers would favour our product over theirs. Overall, declines in statistics observing homeowners' satisfaction with their products would be a good indicator of whether our product was truly successful. I would expect to continue further developing and selling the product until otherwise.
